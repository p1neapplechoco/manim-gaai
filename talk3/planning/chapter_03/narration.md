# Chapter 03 Narration

## Beat 1

[T=00:00] The contact graph says what can happen. It still cannot tell whether the action is right for the task.

## Beat 2

[T=00:11] Tool-environment common sense is relational: tool, object, and environment roles define what the action means.

## Beat 3

[T=00:22] Physical constraints remain the first gate. They decide whether the requested edge can be realized.

## Beat 4

[T=00:33] Semantic constraints add the second gate. The same motion can mean different tasks under different roles.

## Beat 5

[T=00:44] Semantic agents encode those role constraints, extending the library from physically valid to task-valid transitions.

## Beat 6

[T=00:55] The library matrix is a coverage map. Empty cells mark real transfer limits, not cosmetic omissions.

## Beat 7

[T=01:07] After a transition passes both gates, the next question is control. Chapter four turns valid targets into policies.
